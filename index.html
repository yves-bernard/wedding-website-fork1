TOULOUSE

How do I get there?

From Ireland:


Option 1.
Fly into Toulouse airport with Aer Lingus / Ryanair.
Toulouse airport is only 20 minutes from the city centre and 50 minutes from the wedding venue.
Rent a car or download the ‘Bolt’ or ‘Uber’ taxi app to hire a taxi.
If you’re travelling straight to the wedding venue area via taxi, we recommend booking in advance.
Bolt allows pre-booking from 3 days in advance, Uber allows pre-booking 30 days in advance.

Taxis from the airport to Toulouse city centre can be booked on the Bolt / Uber app once you’re through baggage claim.
Alternatively, you can take the shuttle bus to Toulouse city centre from the airport, a one-way ticket is €9:
Airport-shuttle

Option 2.
Fly into Carcassone airport. This is further from the venue (1 hour drive approx.), but you can find good deals. This option works best if you are planning to rent a car.

Option 3.
Take your car on the ferry to Bilbao in Spain and drive 4.5 hours to reach Toulouse. You'll pass through beautiful Basque country and see the Pyrenées in the distance en route.

Transport in and around the venue


The venue is in a restored mill in the countryside and it isn’t very well serviced by public transport.
The Bolt / Uber taxi apps also don’t provide pickups here.

Where to stay in Toulouse city centre?


If you have an extra couple of days and want to explore La Ville Rose, the following areas are nice and central:
Les Carmes, Saint Etienne, Saint Sernin, Saint Cyprien, Saint Georges, Saint Aubin.

What to do in Toulouse:

- Wander the neighbourhoods of Les Carmes, Saint Etienne, Saint Sernin and Saint Cyprien
- Explore the Couvent des Jacobins C14th church and cloister
- Visit 'La Mairie' (the town hall)
- Check out Le Marché Couvert Victor-Hugo
- Hit Les Carmes for bars and restaurants
- Explore Saint Sernin Basilica
- Spend an afternoon at La Halle des Machines (a short bus or taxi ride from the centre)
- Aeroscopia aeroplane museum (15min drive or 1h20min bus from Toulouse)
- Cité de L'Éspace space and space travel museum (short bus or taxi ride from the centre)
- Relax in Le Jardin des Plantes
- Musée des Abbatoirs modern art gallery
- Explore the shops: Rue Jacques Cujas (casual), Rue Saint-Antoine du T (upmarket), Rue Alsace Lorraine (highstreet), (Quartier Libre stationary shop, La Paranthése craft shop, Gallerie de Lafayette, Sephora)

One day trips from Toulouse:

- Carcassonne is a medieval walled city 1hr15 drive from Toulouse, accessible by train.
- Albi is a historic town 1hr drive from Toulouse. If you visit, check out the cathedral and the museum of the artist Toulouse Lautrec.

Two to Three day trips from Toulouse:

- Rent a car to visit Saint-Cirque-Lapopie, Rocamadour and Causses de Quircy Natural Regional Park.
- Take the train to Collioure, a port town on the Mediterranean coast. Visit beaches and towns nearby by foot/by train.
- A 1hr30 drive South to Saint Girons in Arriege is a good base for lots of hikes and walks:
Hikes in Ariege near Saint-Girons.

What to see en route from Bilbao ferry port:

This is a 4-hour drive on the motorway with lots of opportunities for rest stops:
- San Sebastien
- Restaurant Venta Gaxuxa in the tiny seaside town of Bidart.
- Biarritz
- Bayonne is a small lively city with plenty of beautiful Basque-style architecture.
- Lourdes
- Pyrenees National Park; drive to Pont D'Espagne and then hike 1hr to Lake Gaube.
